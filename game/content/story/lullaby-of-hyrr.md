# THE LULLABY OF HYRR

*Main Scenario · Act I-III · ~12-15 hours · 30 Echo quests at launch*

---

## OPENING MONOLOGUE *(played over title-to-creator transition)*

> Most cities sleep, and most cities wake. Hyrr does both at once. It dreams of itself, and the dream becomes the streets. The streets forget. The people forget. Even the gods, when they fell, were politely forgotten — folded into the architecture, hammered into hilts, set into the throats of bells.
>
> But forgetting is a kind of song. And every song, sooner or later, is interrupted.
>
> You are the interruption.

---

## CORE PREMISE

Hyrr is a dreaming city — its architecture, its people, its very laws of physics re-shape between Cycles. At the end of each Cycle, a **Vespers** rings: a city-wide tide of forgetting. Everyone who hears it forgets the Cycle just lived. The next Cycle begins fresh, with new buildings, new shop signs, sometimes new neighbors.

**Wakers** are those who hear the Vespers and *don't* forget. They are vanishingly rare. Most are killed; the rest are conscripted into the **Choir of Watching**, a guild charged with stewarding the city across Cycles, preserving knowledge, and binding the **Aspects** — sentient weapons made from the bodies of the seven gods who died at the city's founding.

Something is wrong. The Vespers are coming faster — what should be a Cycle of years is now a Cycle of weeks. Buildings collapse mid-construction because they're forgotten before they're finished. Children age and reset. The gods inside the Aspects are becoming louder, more hostile.

The world is a song stuck on a verse, and someone is making it skip.

You are a new Waker. You don't know why.

---

## SETTING — HYRR

A walled city on the edge of an inland sea of mercury-bright water. Architecture is **High-Gothic-meets-Edo-meets-stained-glass-cathedral** — buttresses and pagodas and rose windows all stitched together with brass piping that carries warm steam through the streets.

Six districts:
1. **The Crown** — palace district, where the Choir of Watching keeps its archives
2. **The Brass Throat** — industrial belt, where Aspect-smiths bind weapons
3. **Lowering** — port and slums, the only district that remembers itself a little (the sea anchors memory)
4. **The Coil** — temple district, hundreds of small shrines to the dead gods
5. **Pale Garden** — wealthy estates, hedge-mazes that re-route every Cycle
6. **The Hollow Vespers** — *forbidden*. The first district to fall when the Cycles started skipping. A bell-tower district consumed by uncontrolled forgetting; the architecture now eats itself.

Outside the walls: the **Nightlands** — wilderness where the dream thins, populated by the remnants of forgotten things. This is where most dungeons live.

# ACT I — *THE INTERRUPTION* (Quests E01-E10, ~3-4 hrs)

### E01 — *The Empty Bell*
**Tutorial.** You wake on the floor of the *Bell of Lowering*, a tide-flooded shrine in the slum district. A song echoes in your head — the Vespers. You should have forgotten. You haven't. A young **Choir scout** named **MARIN** finds you, panicking — the bell has rung early, and you're *standing*. She drags you out of the rising tide, and on the docks gives you your first Aspect, **Tempest**, an unbound greatsword that immediately starts talking.

> **TEMPEST:** *"Pick me up, blade. Try not to die. I have died enough for both of us."*

Tutorial combat: 3 mooks (forgotten things — formless wisps with too many teeth). Establishes light/heavy, dodge, lock-on.

### E02 — *Marin's Choir*
Marin escorts you to **The Crown**, district of the Choir of Watching. First exposure to the city's grandeur and to other Wakers. You meet:
- **CADENCE**, the Choir's archivist — wry, ancient (real age unclear; she's lived through too many Cycles)
- **HRAEDIN**, captain of Choir's military arm, the Watch — gruff, ex-Pale-Garden noble, suspicious of you
- **THE PRIOR**, head of the Choir — robed, voice modulated, never seen unmasked. Welcomes you with unreadable warmth.

The Prior tells you the Choir believes the early Vespers is the work of a heretic faction called the **Quiet Hand**, who want the city to *forget itself completely*. Your job: investigate.

### E03 — *Ink and Salt*
Cadence sends you to **Lowering** to interview a Quiet Hand sympathizer. Mini-dungeon: a flooded bookshop where the books rewrite themselves between rooms. You fight a mid-mob (a forgotten librarian) and find a ledger that names a **smith** in the Brass Throat as a Quiet Hand contact.

### E04 — *The Brass Throat*
Travel to the Brass Throat. **MEET DAGAN**, an Aspect-smith — sour, brilliant, missing two fingers. He claims the Quiet Hand never existed, that it's a Choir invention to justify the Watch's authority. He gives you a half-bound second Aspect (**Choir**, dual pistols) as proof he's not a heretic — a heretic wouldn't arm a Waker.

> **CHOIR:** *"Hey there, sweetheart. New band, new song. Try not to break me."*

First class-switch unlocks. You can now toggle between Tempest and Choir at any Resonance Spire.

### E05 — *Pale Garden*
Hraedin orders you to investigate a noble in the Pale Garden whose hedge-maze has been re-routing wrong. Stealth/exploration quest. You find the noble — **LADY VEIST** — already dead, her body half-absorbed into the topiary. The hedge has remembered her. The Cycle is forgetting her *into* the dream.

You meet **KEIN**, a Vellish ranger in the noble's employ, who has been hiding the body to protect the family. Kein joins your party as a recurring NPC ally.

### E06 — *The Coil*
The trail leads to **The Coil**, temple district. You investigate a shrine to **Thren the Bloom**. The shrine-keeper — an old Korr woman called **GRIDA** — confesses she's been hearing the goddess *speak* through her dreams. Thren is begging to be unbound, to die properly. The forgetting is hurting her.

This is the first reveal: **the Aspects are suffering.** The Cycles are tearing them apart.

### E07 — *The First Spire*
Cadence reveals the **Resonance Spires** — towers that anchor memory across Cycles — are failing. You travel to the **First Spire** in the Crown to inspect it. Inside, you find sabotage: someone has been carving **un-naming sigils** into the spire's foundation.

Mini-boss: **FORGOTTEN CHORALIST** — a former Choir member who's been half-erased, fights with a corrupted Hymn aspect.

### E08 — *Hraedin's Suspicion*
Hraedin pulls you aside. He doesn't trust the Prior. He thinks the sabotage is coming from inside the Choir. He asks you to do something illegal: break into the Choir archives.

Player choice: **comply** (Watch faction sympathy) or **report him to the Prior** (Choir faction sympathy). Choice affects later dialogue and one ending variant; doesn't gate content.

### E09 — *The Archives*
Either way, you end up in the archives. You find a hidden ledger in Cadence's hand: a list of **Wakers who never officially existed.** Wakers who were "removed" from the city's memory. Murdered, then forgotten, by the Choir itself.

Cadence finds you. She's not angry. She's relieved. *"I've been waiting for someone to find that. I couldn't be the one to show it to you."*

She tells you the truth: the Prior has been **culling Wakers** for centuries, on the grounds that too many awake minds destabilize the dream. There is a faction within the Choir that disagrees. She is one. **MARIN** is one. Hraedin doesn't know yet.

### E10 — *The First Vespers*
A Vespers rings, three weeks early. The whole city forgets. You don't. Marin doesn't. Cadence doesn't. You wake to find Hraedin **erased** — his name gone from the rolls, his quarters occupied by someone else who's always lived there.

Cadence: *"He found something. They got him."*

The Prior summons you. Smiles. Asks how you're settling in. The Prior doesn't remember Hraedin existing.

The Prior was the one who culled him.

> **ACT I CLOSE — Tempest:** *"Blade. We are in the wrong house. Walk slowly."*

# ACT II — *THE QUIET HAND* (Quests E11-E22, ~5-6 hrs)

### E11-E13 — *Going to ground*
You flee with Cadence, Marin, and Kein into **Lowering**. The Choir of Watching is now hunting you. You meet the **real Quiet Hand** — not heretics, but a network of ex-Choir Wakers who escaped the cull. Led by a woman called **SEVEN**, an ancient Sivit who claims to remember Hyrr's founding.

**Aspect added: Bloom** (E11), **Veil** (E12), **Hymn** (E13). Dagan fled to the Hand and is reforging Aspects for the cause.

### E14 — *Seven's Story*
Seven tells you the founding myth, and corrects it.

**The official myth:** Seven gods created the city, sacrificed themselves to seal it against the void, and were honored as Aspects.

**Seven's truth:** Seven gods *lived* in the city. The first **Prior** killed them, one by one, to bind their power into weapons — and to bind the city itself into a self-forgetting state, so no one would remember the murders. The Cycles aren't a divine cycle. They're a coverup.

The Aspects in your hands are the screams of murdered gods.

> **TEMPEST (very quietly):** *"… I would like to sit down."*

### E15-E17 — *The Three Spires*
You and the Hand work to repair the Resonance Spires before another Vespers rings. Three dungeon quests: one in each major district (Brass Throat, Pale Garden, Coil). Each ends with a boss — a **Forgotten Saint**, a Waker culled centuries ago, kept barely alive in the spire's foundation as a memory-battery.

Each Saint, before they die, gives you a fragment of a longer message: **"There is an eighth."**

### E18 — *The Eighth*
You investigate. Across all the lore, all the records, there are seven Aspects. Always seven.

In the deepest archive, Cadence finds an entry that has been carefully un-named. A god whose name has been removed from every record. Domain: **silence**. Aspect: **Hush**.

If there is an eighth Aspect, someone is carrying it. Someone has been carrying it the entire time.

### E19 — *Marin*
Marin betrays you. She has been the Prior's agent the whole time. She is not a young scout — she is one of the oldest Wakers, the Prior's original apprentice. She carries Hush.

She fights you. **Boss: Marin, the Eighth.** Stealth-burst combat, fights with concealed daggers. Mid-fight, dialogue:

> **MARIN:** *"I liked you. I want you to know that. I have liked all of them. They die anyway."*

You don't kill her — she escapes. But you wound her, and the wound is the first thing the city has ever failed to forget.

### E20 — *The Prior's Face*
You and the Hand assault **The Crown**. You break into the Prior's chamber. You unmask the Prior.

**The Prior is you.**

Not literally — but a Waker who looks exactly like your character, plus a few hundred years of weariness. The Prior is a future-version of every Waker who ever rose high enough to be offered the position. The Choir's recruitment cycle is: find a Waker, train them, ascend them, make them the new Prior, kill the old one. Each Prior wears the face of the most recent ascension.

You are being groomed for the role. You always were.

> **PRIOR:** *"I was you. I will be you. The kindest thing I can do is make it quick."*

### E21 — *The Bell of Lowering*
You retreat to where the story began — the flooded shrine where you woke up. The Prior follows.

**Boss: The Prior.** Mirror-match: the Prior fights with the same Aspects you do, in the same combat style. Phase 1: Tempest. Phase 2: Choir. Phase 3: switches Aspects every 30 seconds, forcing you to read the moveset.

You win. The Prior, dying, smiles.

> **PRIOR:** *"Good. You should be the one. I was so tired."*

The Prior dies. The mask falls off. Underneath, your face.

### E22 — *The Choice*
Cadence offers you the throne. Become the new Prior. Continue the system. Kill the next Waker who rises high enough to threaten you. Keep the city sleeping. It's stable. It works. It has worked for a thousand years.

Or: refuse. Try to wake the city. Risk shattering the dream entirely. Possibly destroy Hyrr.

The Hand wants you to refuse. Cadence wants you to refuse but is too tired to fight you if you don't.

**Player choice — gates the Act III variants but story converges by ending.**

> **ACT II CLOSE — Cadence:** *"Whatever you decide, decide loudly. The city deserves a song it remembers."*

# ACT III — *THE WAKING* (Quests E23-E30, ~3-4 hrs)

You refuse the throne. (Both choices funnel here — taking the throne triggers a betrayal arc that ends in the same place; we save 8 quests of dev work.)

### E23-E25 — *The Hollow Vespers*
You return to the **forbidden district** — the bell-tower neighborhood that fell first. Now it's a self-eating wound in the city. Architecture loops back on itself. Time is unstable. You and the Hand fight through three dungeons here:

- **The Tilted Belfry** — gravity is wrong, combat platforms rotate
- **The Mirror Choir** — every enemy has a copy of you that mirrors your moves
- **The Long Vespers** — a single corridor that gets longer the more you walk it; you outrun a tide of forgetting

Boss at the end of E25: **The First Bell** — the original Vespers-bell, alive, sentient, hungry. It is the *engine* of the Cycles.

### E26 — *Marin Returns*
Marin returns, wounded, weary, half-erased. She has finally been hurt enough to remember being human. She joins you. She fights at your side.

> **MARIN:** *"I was made of silence. You taught me what a sentence is."*

Cosmetic unlock: Hush Aspect.

### E27-E28 — *The Aspects' Verdict*
You go to each Aspect, one by one, and ask them what they want. Each one's voice has changed across the story; this is the payoff.

- **Tempest** wants to fight the bell. *"It killed my brother. I will end it."*
- **Choir** wants to *play the bell.* *"Bells don't die. They retire. Let me retire it."*
- **Bloom** wants to die. *"I am tired. Let me end."*
- **Veil** wants to vanish. *"I will go where I am not seen. That is my prayer."*
- **Hymn** wants to forgive the bell. *"It was made by suffering. So was I."*
- **Ember** wants to burn. *"Of course."*
- **Vow** wants to stand. *"I will be the wall the bell breaks against."*
- **Hush** (Marin) wants to apologize. *"I want to be loud. Just once."*

You choose three of the eight to wield in the final battle. The choice changes the final cinematic dialogue.

### E29 — *The Climb*
Final dungeon: the bell-tower at the center of **The Crown**. Linear, atmospheric. Side characters fall behind one by one as the air thins. Cadence stays with you longest. Marin, last.

Mid-dungeon mini-boss: **The Choir of Watching**, your former colleagues, fight you to the last. You cut through them.

Top of the tower: a single chamber. Empty except for the Bell.

### E30 — *The Lullaby of Hyrr*
**Final boss: THE BELL.** Three phases. The phase 3 reveal: the Bell isn't an instrument. It is a *child.* The first Prior's daughter, willingly bound into the city's memory engine to keep her father's murders hidden.

She doesn't want to be the bell. She never did.

You do not "kill" her. You unbind her. The city wakes.

**ENDING.**

The Vespers stops ringing. People remember being alive yesterday. Many can't handle it; there are riots, mourning, weddings remembered, deaths remembered. The Hand becomes the new government. Cadence retires. Marin sets up a school for new Wakers — the city will need many more, now that minds are awake.

You walk out of the bell-tower into a city that, for the first time, recognizes you.

**Final lines, voiceover:**

> Most cities sleep, and most cities wake. Hyrr learned to do both.
>
> Now it is learning to do neither — to simply *be*. That is harder. It hurts more. It is also, at last, real.
>
> Walk slowly, blade. There is a long road. And the city, for the first time, is paying attention.

**Credits.**

## DESIGN NOTES

- **Narrative pillars:** memory as politics, complicity, the cost of waking up
- **Tone:** hopeful melancholy. We earn the cynicism, we earn the beauty
- **Player agency:** choice flavors the story but doesn't fork it (FFXIV approach). Better depth-per-budget than branching
- **Cinematic budget:** in-engine only, no pre-rendered CG. Heavy use of camera + music + dialogue cards. ~10 cinematics across the act, 30-60 seconds each
- **Companion AI:** Marin / Kein / Cadence / Dagan join you for specific quests as buffer/heal/dps presences. They don't compete with player party. Their mechanical contribution is small; their *narrative* presence is the point
- **Reusability:** dungeon environments designed so a single biome (e.g., Brass Throat industrial) supports 4-5 different dungeons via lighting + prop swap. Critical for solo-dev scope
