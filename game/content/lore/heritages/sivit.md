# SIVIT — The Long Listeners

> *"We do not remember more. We listen longer."*

The Sivit are tall, slender, long-eared. The closest analogue to FFXIV's Elezen / FFXI's Elvaan. But where most fantasy elf-analogues are *aloof,* the Sivit are **attentive** — patient, watchful, almost theatrically polite. They are the heritage most closely associated with the Choir of Watching, for better and worse.

---

## PHYSICAL GUIDELINES

- **Height range:** 180-215cm (taller than Hjari ceiling)
- **Build:** consistently slender; even muscular Sivit read as wiry rather than bulky. Wide muscle slider should *not* push past Hjari-equivalent
- **Skin tones:** cool palette dominant (porcelain, slate, ash-blue undertones, deep cool brown), but warm tones available
- **Eyes:** larger than Hjari; iris colors trend toward unusual (pale gray, lavender, gold). Heterochromia at higher rate
- **Ears:** **long, slightly upswept,** ~2-3x Hjari ear length. Critical signature — animate gently when emoting (small twitch on listen-emote)
- **Hair:** trends toward straight or wavy; very long styles available; classical updos signature

Sivit base mesh is the **height variant** — same bone hierarchy as Hjari, scaled. Animations need a single retarget pass.

## CULTURE

The Sivit are the city's archivists, lawyers, court musicians, and Choir clergy. They run the academies and the libraries. Their cultural defining gesture is **the listening hold** — a small inclination of the head with one hand at the temple, palm open, indicating *I am giving you my full attention. Speak.*

It is rude in Sivit culture to interrupt. It is rude to fail to acknowledge. It is rude to forget what someone said three weeks ago. Memory is a cultural virtue, which makes the Cycles feel especially personal to the Sivit — a Cycle ending is a city-wide *insult*.

**Sivit-Choir alignment:** historically, the Sivit are over-represented in the Choir of Watching. Many Sivit families have served the Choir for ten or more Cycles. This is not unanimous — the Quiet Hand has many Sivit defectors who couldn't stomach the cull. But a player Sivit Waker will get extra suspicion and extra recognition from Choir NPCs.

## NAMING CONVENTIONS

Two-part names. A given name and a *house-line* — a long matriline-or-patriline chain shortened by everyday speech.

**Given names:** musical, multi-syllable, tend to end in -el, -ir, -ane, -on, -us.
*Examples:* Aerelin, Ostavir, Cyranel, Therion, Vesselon, Maerin, Sereth, Ilaeon, Vyrane, Calyssa.

**House-lines:** styled like *"of House [name]"* in formal address; abbreviated in casual.
*Examples:* House Caerwynd, House Ottavar, House Ivelyn, House Thenarine, House Soressi.

**Casual address:** given name only. Formal address: full name. Elder-formal: house first, then given.

**Full name examples:**
- Aerelin of House Caerwynd
- Ostavir of House Ottavar
- Calyssa of House Soressi (called *Calyssa* in chat, *Calyssa Soressi* by acquaintances, *Soressi-Calyssa* by elders)

## VOICE TONES

1. **Cool-low** — Choir-archivist, measured
2. **Bright-clear-mid** — court-singer, classically trained
3. **Soft-high** — academic, careful
4. **Warm-low** — old-house elder, tired but kind
5. **Sharp-mid** — duelist, theater-trained
6. **Whispered-mid** — librarian, conspiratorial
7. **Sing-song-mid** — bard, poetic cadence
8. **Hushed-low** — exiled noble, world-weary

Sivit lines should sit *just slightly* on the formal side of Hjari equivalents. They tend toward longer sentences in combat barks.

## SIGNATURE BACKSTORIES

**The Archivist's Apprentice** — you grew up in the Crown's libraries, taught to remember verbatim. The Cycles felt to you like books being torn from shelves. → affinity **Hymn**

**The Inheritor** — your house has served the Choir for nine generations. You discovered the cull as a child. You said nothing for ten years. Then you Woke. → affinity **Vow** (oath bound, then broken)

**The Long-Eared Witness** — you can hear a half-mile better than a Hjari. You have heard things across the Cycles that no one was supposed to hear. → affinity **Hush** (locked, post-launch reveal, set during quiz)

**The Heretic** — your house disowned you when you joined the Quiet Hand. You walked across the Pale Garden barefoot to prove you didn't need them. The dream remembers your bare feet. → affinity **Tempest** (defiance, motion)

## RP FLAVOR HOOKS

- Sivit greet each other with the **listening hold** described above
- Sivit speak full sentences. They distrust slang. They permit it in others
- They have a tradition called **The Hour** — once a week, a Sivit will sit silently for an hour, listening to the city. Many Choir cells map to which Sivit holds The Hour at which time
- They are physically expressive in subtle ways: ear position, finger position, gaze direction. A Sivit emote should always include some ear/hand component
- They distrust the Brass Throat. The forges are *too loud.* They like Lowering — the sea is patient
