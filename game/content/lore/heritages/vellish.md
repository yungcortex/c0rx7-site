# VELLISH — The Walked-In

> *"We came in from the Nightlands. We may not always stay."*

The Vellish are beastfolk — feline or lupine sub-builds, with animated tails and ears, slit pupils, and longer canines. They are the city's outsiders by self-conception: every Vellish family has a story of having walked in from the Nightlands at some point in living-cycle memory. Many still keep one foot beyond the walls.

The Vellish are the **rangers, scouts, hunters, couriers, and most of the Watch's frontline.** They are over-represented in dangerous professions and in the Quiet Hand.

---

## PHYSICAL GUIDELINES

- **Height range:** 165-200cm (between Hjari and Sivit)
- **Build:** lean, athletic, predator-poised. Muscular slider available but trends visible-cut rather than dense
- **Skin tones:** entire palette, often patterned (subtle stripes / spots in feline sub-build, undercoat shading in lupine)
- **Eyes:** **slit pupils** mandatory. Iris colors trend toward bright (amber, green, gold, ice-blue). Glow effect on eye reads strongly
- **Ears:** **animal ears at top of head** — feline (triangular, alert) or lupine (rounded, slightly larger). Animate constantly: idle twitch, perk on listen, flatten on aggression. Critical for tonal read
- **Tail:** mandatory. Length / curl / fluff sliders. Animates with mood and idle
- **Teeth:** longer canines visible in open-mouth emotes
- **Hair:** any human style works, but Vellish often integrate hair-and-ear styling (braids around ears, fringes that frame ear bases)

### Sub-build flavor
- **Feline-Vellish:** narrower frame, more agile silhouette, ear angle more vertical
- **Lupine-Vellish:** broader chest, more grounded posture, ear angle more lateral

(There is informal Vellish lore about "Other-shaped" Vellish — non-feline, non-lupine ancestry — but for v1 we keep to the two sub-builds.)

---

## CULTURE

The Vellish are the city's **half-citizens by choice.** Every Vellish family carries the institutional memory of having entered the city, which means every Vellish family carries the option of leaving. Most don't. The ones who left often come back.

**The Walk-In:** a Vellish coming-of-age ritual. At 17-19, a Vellish leaves the city for the Nightlands for one full Cycle (or, post-story, one full season). They survive on their own. They return when they choose. Some return with stories. Some return with company. Some don't return.

**Vellish hunting culture:** Vellish hunt the forgotten things of the Nightlands the way Hjari fish the inland sea. The Vellish are the heritage most likely to recognize an Aspect-resonant beast in the wild before a Choir scholar does.

**Vellish-Quiet-Hand alignment:** the Hand has a high Vellish presence because the Vellish are *suspicious of the Choir's stability narrative.* The Choir says *the Cycles are necessary.* The Vellish, who've come from outside, say *we lived without them. They are not necessary.* The Choir resents the Vellish. The Watch (formally part of the Choir) is mostly Vellish. There is institutional tension here that surfaces in story moments.

## NAMING CONVENTIONS

Vellish use **single given names plus a place-of-walk-in**, even for Vellish born inside the city. The place-of-walk-in is inherited from the family ancestor who entered Hyrr.

**Given names:** sharp consonants, short, animal-resonant; sometimes phonetically close to a beast-call.
*Examples:* Kein, Naya, Vesh, Ren, Talin, Korra, Iro, Sune, Mara, Drak, Rhae, Yael, Toren.

**Place-of-walk-in:** descriptive, often poetic.
*Examples:* of-the-Bright-Bowl, of-the-Hollow-Birch, of-the-Long-Light, of-the-Ten-Stones, of-the-Cold-Spring, of-the-First-Snow.

**Full name examples:**
- Kein of-the-Hollow-Birch
- Naya of-the-Cold-Spring
- Talin of-the-Long-Light
- Drak of-the-Ten-Stones

A Vellish who **takes a city-place** as their walk-in line (e.g., *Ren of-the-Brass-Throat*) is making a cultural statement: they consider themselves done walking in. This is sometimes celebrated and sometimes mocked.

## VOICE TONES

1. **Wary-mid** — scout, alert, low resting volume
2. **Husky-low** — hunter, casual
3. **Bright-sharp** — courier, fast-talking
4. **Soft-warm** — outsider-poet, gentle
5. **Growled-low** — Watch-officer, intimidating
6. **Wry-mid** — bartender, knowing
7. **Distant-mid** — exile, contemplative
8. **Snarl-low** — Quiet-Hand-radical, barely-restrained

Vellish lines often **trail.** Sentences end with a small drop. This is a vocal tic of the heritage — they leave room for the listener.

## SIGNATURE BACKSTORIES

**The Walked-In** — you yourself walked in from the Nightlands during your last Walk-In. You stayed. The dream is still half-strange to you. → affinity **Veil** (secrets, intimacy)

**The Watch Officer** — you joined the Watch young. You served honorably. You learned what was being hidden. You're considering desertion. → affinity **Tempest** (oath, edge)

**The Hunter** — you tracked forgotten things in the Nightlands. You learned to recognize a god's footprint. One of the gods began following you home. → affinity **Bloom** (the harvest, the ending)

**The Courier** — you ran messages between districts faster than the Watch could intercept. The Quiet Hand recruited you when you were eleven. → affinity **Choir** (distance, music, timing)

## RP FLAVOR HOOKS

- Vellish greet by **a quick lateral head-tilt** — almost a feline check, almost a sniff. Performed even by lupine sub-build
- Vellish **walk barefoot indoors** when they trust the host. Wearing shoes in another Vellish's home is a small insult
- Vellish track the **moon and the inland sea's tides** even inside the city walls. Moonless nights make Vellish quiet
- Vellish are the heritage with the **strongest oral storytelling tradition.** Vellish tavern-evenings often turn into multi-hour story rounds. The stories tend to be true and long
- Vellish distrust the Pale Garden (claustrophobic). They distrust the Coil (closed-in). They love the Brass Throat (open and useful), the docks, and the rooftops of the Crown — they like to perch
- Vellish ear position is the heritage's primary emote tell. A Vellish whose ears are pinned back in casual conversation is *furious.* A Vellish whose ears swivel mid-sentence has heard something you didn't

---

*Vellish should feel like the heritage that came in from outside and might walk back out. The player who picks Vellish is choosing to be both inside and not.*
