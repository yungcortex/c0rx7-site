# KORR — The Stone-Heavy

> *"The Cycles forget the city. They cannot forget us."*

The Korr are short, broad, deeply rooted. Two sub-builds: **Tall-Korr** (160-180cm, broad and dense like a Norse miner) and **Short-Korr** (120-145cm, compact and stubborn like a Tarutaru). Both share the same heritage culture, the same naming, the same voice texture. The two builds intermarry constantly. The Korr think it's funny that other heritages think of them as two peoples.

The Korr are the heritage *least* affected by the Cycles. No one knows why. Theories range from *something in their blood* to *they simply refuse.*

---

## PHYSICAL GUIDELINES

### Tall-Korr
- **Height range:** 160-180cm
- **Build:** broad-shouldered, thick-limbed, density-favoring. Muscular slider goes higher than any other heritage
- **Skin tones:** warm dominant (deep amber, copper, dark olive, mahogany), with cool grays available
- **Hair:** thick, often braided. Beards available across the masc/fem slider — Korr hair grows everywhere, equally
- **Eyes:** smaller than Hjari, deep-set, often warm-toned (amber, hazel, dark brown)

### Short-Korr
- **Height range:** 120-145cm
- **Build:** compact, surprisingly dense; muscular slider available; not childlike — Korr proportions even at small height read as adult
- Otherwise same palette/feature options as Tall-Korr
- **Mesh authoring:** ideally a separate base mesh (different proportions enough that morphing from Tall to Short reads ugly). Both meshes share a common skeleton; bone scaling does the rest

Korr should be **distinctly stocky** in silhouette. The character creator should make this difficult to undo — the "thin Korr" should still read as Korr, not as a thin Hjari.

---

## CULTURE

The Korr are the city's **stoneworkers, herbalists, brewmasters, midwives, and undertakers.** Anywhere there is a generations-long craft, you find Korr. They run the Coil's temples (most temple-keepers are Korr), most of the Pale Garden's hedge work (the topiary masters), and a sizeable share of Brass Throat's Aspect-smithing.

**Korr defining cultural value:** *unstuckness.* You cannot move a Korr. You cannot intimidate one. You cannot, the saying goes, *forget* one. The Vespers tides over a Korr family the way water tides over a stone — it changes the surface and leaves the stone.

**Korr longhouses** are the city's most enduring architecture. Many Korr houses have stood through five or more Cycles. When the city re-shapes itself, Korr houses tend to remain in roughly the same location with roughly the same floor plan. Choir scholars have long studied this and found nothing useful. The Korr say: *"We just live there. The house knows."*

**Tall and Short:** the two sub-builds are not socially separated. A Korr couple of mixed heights is unremarkable. Children's heights are unpredictable. There are no jokes about it that aren't told within Korr families themselves.

## NAMING CONVENTIONS

One-part names plus a craft-line. Korr use **single given names** (often two-syllable, hard consonants), and append the family's craft.

**Given names:** earthy, hard-syllable, frequently ending in -ek, -in, -ot, -al, -un.
*Examples:* Brak, Hilda, Gridan, Marek, Tova, Onek, Stelin, Vorga, Hessa, Korot, Iva, Druja.

**Craft-lines:** *"of the [craft]"*. The craft is the family's craft, not necessarily the bearer's profession.
*Examples:* of the Stone, of the Press, of the Hearth, of the Spade, of the Forge, of the Loom, of the Wash, of the Bell.

**Full name examples:**
- Brak of the Stone
- Hilda of the Press
- Gridan of the Bell
- Onek of the Loom

A Korr who **abandons their craft-line** has done something culturally serious. It implies severance from family. The few canonical Korr characters in the story who go by single names — *Grida, Dagan* — are usually marked by something significant.

## VOICE TONES

1. **Deep-warm** — eldest grandmother energy, calm
2. **Gravelly-low** — stonemason, pleasant
3. **Bright-mid (Short-Korr trend)** — sharp, alert
4. **Dry-low** — undertaker, kind humor
5. **Booming-low** — temple-keeper
6. **Soft-warm** — midwife, attentive
7. **Wry-mid** — brewmaster, social
8. **Steady-low** — herbalist, observant

Korr lines should be *unhurried.* Korr never rush a sentence. Even battle barks read as deliberate.

## SIGNATURE BACKSTORIES

**The Last in the House** — your craft-line ended with you. The house still stands. You inherited a doorway you can't close. → affinity **Vow** (the weight, the holding)

**The Brewmaster** — you ran a tavern in Lowering. You knew everyone. You watched the same names disappear and reappear under different faces, Cycle by Cycle, and you started writing them down on the inside of barrels. → affinity **Bloom** (witnessing, ending)

**The Midwife** — you delivered children whose names the Cycles forgot. You did not. You named them privately, in a journal you keep stoneproof. → affinity **Hymn** (mercy)

**The Defiant** — you woke from the first Vespers you ever heard. You walked into the Choir's office and demanded to be put to work. They didn't know what to do with you. → affinity **Tempest**

## RP FLAVOR HOOKS

- Korr greet by **clasping forearms** — palm to inside of elbow. Long Korr, short Korr, doesn't matter; the gesture is the same
- Korr **never lie about food.** Lying about food is a moral failing. Other lies are negotiable
- Korr **toast formally,** even at small meals. *"To what stands."* is a common toast
- Korr distrust the Pale Garden (showy). They distrust the Crown (decorative). They like the Brass Throat (honest), Lowering (honest), and the Coil (their houses)
- Korr hold grudges across Cycles. The Hjari memorial stones can preserve names; Korr **memory is direct.** Some Korr remember things their grandparents did before the last three Vespers. No one quite knows how they do it. The Korr don't think it's interesting

---

*Korr should feel like the heritage that the city cannot break. The player who picks Korr is choosing to be heavy on the world.*
