# HJARI — The Carried

> *"We were here when the bell first rang. We will be here when it stops."*

The Hjari are humanlike — the city's most populous heritage, balanced in build and stat distribution. The closest analogue to FFXIV's Hyur or generic high-fantasy human, but with their own specific cultural texture rather than generic "default."

---

## PHYSICAL GUIDELINES

- **Height range:** 160-190cm (sliders)
- **Build:** broad spectrum; no signature silhouette
- **Skin tones:** the full palette (warm browns, ochres, cool whites, deep blacks); Hjari are mixed by definition
- **Eyes:** typical human iris colors plus the faint Waker-glow toggle
- **Ears:** rounded, modest. No exaggeration
- **Hair:** any texture, any color; the Hjari are *cosmetic-flexible* by design

The Hjari base mesh should be the most "rigging-conservative" — animations test on Hjari first. Other heritages should retarget cleanly.

---

## CULTURE

The Hjari are the city's labor and its memory. They run the docks, the foundries, the markets, and the Choir's lower ranks. Most of Hyrr's heroes and most of its dead come from Hjari families.

**Family is the architecture of identity.** Hjari name themselves with three given names — one chosen by parents, one chosen by the family elder at age 14, one chosen by the bearer at age 21 (or whenever they Wake). When a Hjari Wakes, they almost always change their third name — sometimes to mark the rupture, sometimes to bind themselves to who they were before.

**Hjari memorial tradition:** when a Hjari dies, their family carves their three names into a household stone. The stone is passed down. Most Hjari homes have a wall of these stones, going back a dozen Cycles or more. The stones survive Vespers because they are *touched daily.* (This becomes a plot point: the stones are how Hjari families resist the forgetting. They are also why the Choir of Watching has, historically, targeted Hjari Wakers more aggressively than other heritages.)

## NAMING CONVENTIONS

Three-part names. First given by parents, second by family elder at 14, third self-chosen at 21.

**Parent-given (first names):** soft, vowel-heavy, often inherited from the inland sea's old fishing villages.
*Examples:* Aleo, Mirin, Salka, Tovan, Eira, Joran, Vesh, Lin, Cael, Anoa.

**Elder-given (second names):** virtues or tools. Pronounced ceremonial-formal.
*Examples:* of-the-Steady-Hand, of-the-Ash-Forge, of-the-Open-Door, of-the-Long-Watch, of-the-Salted-Knife, of-the-Open-Field.

**Self-chosen (third names):** anything. Most pick a single short word that means something to them.
*Examples:* Bell, Quiet, Threnody, Verge, Iron, Hush, Lantern.

**Full name examples:**
- Mirin of-the-Ash-Forge Bell
- Tovan of-the-Long-Watch Lantern
- Salka of-the-Salted-Knife Iron

For player UI, only the **third name** is the displayed name in chat. Full names appear in cinematic dialogue and inspection.

## VOICE TONES

8 voice sets per heritage × 5 pitch variants. For Hjari:

1. **Warm-low** — labor-class warmth, working-vocabulary
2. **Sharp-high** — quick, witty, market-trader energy
3. **Soft-mid** — Choir-trained, careful, ceremonial
4. **Rough-low** — dock-worker, laconic
5. **Bright-mid** — youthful, optimistic
6. **Dry-low** — academic, disillusioned
7. **Husky-mid** — singer's voice, Lowering port-tavern
8. **Clipped-high** — military, ex-Watch

Each set has ~20 combat barks, 12 emote vocalizations, 8 Aspect-binding lines.

## SIGNATURE BACKSTORIES *(creator quiz weights)*

The 4-question backstory quiz seeds starting Aspect affinity + opening monologue variant. For Hjari, the most-fitting backstories:

**The Stone-Walker** — your family kept a memorial wall. You touched the stones every morning. When you Woke, the stones remembered *you* before you remembered them. → starts with affinity for **Vow** (oaths, weight)

**The Foundry Apprentice** — you worked the Brass Throat. You made parts of weapons that ended up Aspects. You suspect your hands are why one of them speaks to you. → starts with affinity for **Tempest** (forge, edge)

**The Dock Singer** — you sang in Lowering's tide-shrines. The sea anchored your memory. The Vespers passed and you remembered the song. → starts with affinity for **Hymn** (mercy, breath)

**The Errand Child** — youngest of a large family, you ran messages across all six districts. You knew the city like a body. When the city forgot, you didn't, because you had memorized the inside of it. → starts with affinity for **Choir** (distance, music)

## RP FLAVOR HOOKS

- Hjari speak about death frequently, lightly. It is a normalized presence.
- They are casually religious. Most carry a small token of one of the Aspects — a coin, a string, a piece of broken glass.
- They have a polite phrase when meeting a fellow Waker: *"You're carrying. I see."*
- They mistrust the Pale Garden. They mistrust the Choir of Watching. They like the Quiet Hand even when they don't say so.
- They are the heritage with the most gallows humor, and the most working songs.

---

*Hjari should feel like the heart of the city. Not the most exotic, but the most lived-in. The player who picks Hjari is choosing the heritage that belongs.*
